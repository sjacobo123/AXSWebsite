History

Founders of the Fraternity
Top row – T. Howard Mathews, Edward Mattke, B.E. McCormick, James Silverthorn
Bottom row – Joseph G. Holty, Harold Eggers, Frank J. Petura, A.E. Kundert

Alpha Chi Sigma was founded upon the three purposes, in 1902 at the University of Wisconsin by Joseph Gerard Holty, Joseph Howard Mathews, Bart Eldred McCormick, Raymond Tracy Conger, Frank Joseph Petura, James Chisholm Silverthorn, Alfred Emil Kundert, Alexander Gustav Mattke and Harold Everett Eggers. Following its formation, the organization expanded and established chapters all over the nation. In 1971, the first women were initiated, tranforming the organization into a co-ed professional fraternity. The Alpha Alpha Chapter was originally founded in 1916. Unfortunately, a few years after its creation, the chapter dissolved.

Thankfully, because of the efforts of 5 very passionate students (which later became the alpha class), the chapter was reformed and worked towards reactivation in 2011. With the help of the Sigma Chapter, the Alpha Chi Sigma chapter in Berekeley, the chapter at Stanford was able to follow all the neccessary criteria to eventually be reinstated in 2014 as a nationally recognized chapter: Alpha Alpha Chapter. The picture above has the alpha, beta, gamma, and delta classes formed during the chapter's colony phase with the plaque recognizing the group as an officially recognized chapter.
